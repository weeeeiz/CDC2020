Analysis

Generally speaking, for each individual location, the amounts of air pollutants have increasing trends over time. This doesn't necessarily mean that the number of fires increased, since those pollutants can be cumulated over time.

Across San Francisco, locations to the east were more polluted than those places closer to the sea. This matches the map of fires -- inland cities had more fires than coast cities. Another possible reason could be that some of the pollutants (nitrogen dioxide and trioxygen, for instance) can resolve in water.

Tableau

I picked four pollutants from the dataset to visualize. Using Tableau, I am able to present how these four pollutants change in amounts over the past three months.
